§ 1 — Overview: Procurement for Experimental Purposes

LEGAL DISCLAIMER This guide is a reference tool, not legal authority. Always verify citations against current statute, policy memos, and organizational guidance. Coordinate all determinations with SSC/JA. The Contracting Officer or Agreements Officer retains full responsibility for all decisions. Last updated: March 2026.

1.1 What Is 10 U.S.C. § 4023?

10 U.S.C. § 4023 (formerly § 2373) authorizes the Secretary of Defense and the Secretaries of the Military Departments to purchase supplies for experimental or test purposes in the development of the best supplies needed for national defense. This authority traces back to the Air Corps Act of 1926 and has been called "The Forgotten Authority."

Covered Items (as amended by FY2026 NDAA § 1831): Demonstrations, prototypes, products, supplies, parts, accessories, auxiliary services, and designs for defense-related articles. The FY2026 NDAA modernized the authority from an enumerated list of domains to broader language covering virtually any defense-related item, including software, AI, and non-traditional technologies.

Legacy Domains (pre-NDAA 2026): Ordnance, signal, chemical activity, transportation, energy, medical, space-flight, telecommunications, and aeronautical supplies.

Cite: 10 U.S.C. § 4023(a), as amended by § 1831 of P.L. 119-60 (FY2026 NDAA, Dec. 18, 2025); formerly amended by Pub. L. 114-92, Pub. L. 115-232; renumbered from § 2373 by Pub. L. 116-283 § 1841(b)(1)

SSC Relevance: "Space-flight" is an explicitly covered domain, making this authority particularly relevant to SSC's mission. Space-related supplies, components, sensors, communications equipment, and software enabling experimentation all fall within the statutory scope.

1.2 Why Use This Authority?

  • Speed: Non-FAR route demonstrated from requirement to award in under 2 weeks, with some cases in under 1 week
  • Flexibility: Can be sole source without J&A; informal evaluation methods (oral presentations, emails) permitted
  • Low Protest Risk: Non-FAR awards arguably outside GAO jurisdiction (see Air Tractor decision, fn. 2)
  • Broad Eligibility: Traditional contractors, nontraditional contractors, and foreign entities all eligible
  • No Dollar Ceiling: Statute contains no dollar limitation; only quantities must be limited to experimental need
  • Congressional Preference: Congress directed DoD to establish a preference for using Sections 4021, 4022, and 4023 for S&T and prototyping programs

1.3 Two Primary Routes

● Non-FAR Route (Primary)

  • FAR/DFARS do not apply
  • No CICA competition required
  • No J&A required
  • Flexible instrument (commercial contract, OT-like agreement, custom)
  • Arguably outside GAO protest jurisdiction
  • Award timeline: Days to 2 weeks

● FAR-Based Route

  • Standard FAR/DFARS procedures apply
  • Section 4023 cited as authority on cover page
  • D&F supports sole source basis (FAR 6.103, formerly 6.302)
  • Standard FAR contract instrument
  • Within GAO/COFC protest jurisdiction
  • Award timeline: 6–15 weeks

Cite: 10 U.S.C. § 4023(b) ("Chapter 137 applies only when...purchases are made in quantities greater than necessary"); DoD OT Guide (July 2023); GAO Decision B-418244 (Air Tractor, Feb. 10, 2020)

§ 2 — Statutory Text & Legal Framework

2.1 Full Statutory Text

10 U.S.C. § 4023 — Procurement for Experimental Purposes

FY2026 NDAA Amendment (P.L. 119-60, § 1831): Section 1831 of the FY2026 NDAA significantly amended § 4023. The statutory text below reflects the pre-amendment version. Key changes include: (1) expanded covered categories from enumerated domains to broader "demonstrations, prototypes, products, supplies, parts, accessories, auxiliary services, and designs for defense-related articles"; (2) explicit authorization of prototyping as an experimental purpose; (3) authorization of contract modifications under this authority; and (4) streamlined experiment-to-fielding transition. Verify exact amended language in the enrolled bill (P.L. 119-60) as U.S. Code classifications are still pending.

(a) Authority (pre-amendment text). — The Secretary of Defense and the Secretaries of the military departments may each buy ordnance, signal, chemical activity, transportation, energy, medical, space-flight, telecommunications, and aeronautical supplies, including parts and accessories, and designs thereof, that the Secretary considers necessary for experimental or test purposes in the development of the best supplies that are needed for the national defense.

(b) Procedures. — Purchases under this section may be made inside or outside the United States and by contract or otherwise. Chapter 137 of this title applies only when such purchases are made in quantities greater than necessary for experimentation, technical evaluation, assessment of operational utility, or safety or to provide a residual operational capability.

Source: 10 U.S.C. § 4023, as amended by § 1831 of P.L. 119-60 (FY2026 NDAA, Dec. 18, 2025). U.S. Code classifications pending final update. Consult enrolled bill for exact amended text.

Key Legal Interpretation (Subsection (b)): Chapter 137 of Title 10 incorporates the Armed Services Procurement Act (ASPA) — the fundamental basis of FAR and DFARS. The statute exempts purchases from Chapter 137 when made in quantities necessary for experimentation, technical evaluation, assessment of operational utility, safety, or residual operational capability. This exemption is what enables the Non-FAR route.

2.2 What "Experimental Purposes" Means

  • Government experimentation: The Government must use the supplies for experimental/test purposes — the contractor need not experiment during performance
  • Conventional items qualify: Items purchased need not themselves be experimental; conventional items purchased to enable government experimentation are covered
  • Acceptable purposes: Experimentation, technical evaluation, assessment of operational utility, safety testing, prototyping (added by FY2026 NDAA), demonstrations (added by FY2026 NDAA), and residual operational capability
  • Quantity limitation: Must be limited to quantities necessary for the stated experimental purpose
  • Modifications authorized: FY2026 NDAA § 1831 explicitly authorizes modifications under this authority
  • Experiment-to-fielding: FY2026 NDAA streamlines transition from experimentation to fielding of successful items

Cite: GAO Decision B-418244 (Air Tractor, Feb. 10, 2020) — "the relevant test is whether the Government will use the supplies for experimental purposes, not whether the contractor experiments during performance"

PM Expectation-Setting: If using the Non-FAR route, your mission partner PM should expect a streamlined process with minimal documentation overhead. If using the FAR-based route, set expectations for a traditional acquisition timeline with formal documentation. Either way, the D&F is the critical document — ensure the experimental purpose is clearly articulated.
Revolutionary FAR Overhaul (RFO) Impact: EO 14275 (Apr. 2025) and OMB M-25-26 initiated a comprehensive FAR rewrite. The FAR is being streamlined to statutory-only provisions; non-statutory content is moving to nonmandatory "buying guides" (Strategic Acquisition Guidance). DoD class deviations (DPCAP, Dec. 2025) have renumbered and simplified key FAR parts. For the FAR-based route, note: FAR Part 6 competition sections are renumbered (6.302 → 6.103); SAT raised to $350K; MPT raised to $15K; Part 12 now consolidates commercial procedures from former Part 13; FAR Part 52 reduced by ~30%. SAF/AQC guidance has made the DAFFARS nonmandatory, giving COs greater discretion. All FAR citations in this guide reflect RFO changes where known.

§ 7 — Delegation of Authority

7.1 Delegation Chain

10 U.S.C. § 4023 vests authority in the Secretary of Defense and the Secretaries of the Military Departments. This authority is delegated through the DAF SCO chain per DAF PM 22-C-03.

  • SSC SCO: Has been delegated authority to approve § 4023 D&Fs per DAF PM 22-C-03
  • The SSC SCO delegation is confirmed and active — no further delegation action is required to execute a § 4023 procurement at SSC/PK
  • For reference, other organizations with delegated authority include DARPA and the Navy

Cite: DAF PM 22-C-03 (SCO Delegation of 10 USC 4023); DAF Alternate Authorities Guide (March 2025)

SSC/PK Status: Delegation Confirmed. The SSC SCO holds delegated authority to approve D&Fs under 10 U.S.C. § 4023. The delegation letter is on file. Contracting Officers and Agreements Officers at SSC/PK may proceed with § 4023 procurements following SCO D&F approval.

7.2 D&F Approval Authority

The D&F is the primary approval document for § 4023 procurements. At SSC/PK, the approval chain is:

  • Prepared by: CO/AO and PM (co-signed)
  • Reviewed by: SSC/JA (recommended)
  • Approved by: SSC SCO (per DAF PM 22-C-03 delegated authority)

Cite: DAF PM 22-C-03
